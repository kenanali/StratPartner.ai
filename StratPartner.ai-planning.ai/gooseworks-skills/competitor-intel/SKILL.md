# Competitor Intelligence

## When to Use
- "Who are my competitors and what are they doing?"
- "Track what [competitor] is saying/doing"
- "Find gaps in competitor positioning I can exploit"

## Setup — Identify Competitors
Before any research, ask the user: "Which competitors do you want to track?"
Never hardcode or assume competitor names.

Categories to explore:
- **Direct**: Same ICP, same use case (e.g. other AI strategy tools)
- **Indirect**: Adjacent tools the ICP uses instead (e.g. Notion + ChatGPT, Miro, Dovetail)
- **Status quo**: What they do today without any tool (e.g. junior consultant + Google Docs)

## Phase 1 — One-Time Competitor Profile
For each competitor, research:

**Positioning & Messaging**
- Homepage headline and subheadline
- How they describe their ICP
- Key value claims
- What they never say (reveals blind spots)

**Content & SEO**
- Blog topics and frequency (search: `site:[domain] blog`)
- Keywords they rank for (search: `[competitor] site:ahrefs.com` or use SEMrush/Ubersuggest)
- Paid ad presence (search: `[competitor] ad`)

**Social & Community**
- LinkedIn page activity
- Founder LinkedIn presence and posting themes
- Twitter/X activity
- Reddit mentions: `site:reddit.com [competitor name]`

**Customer Voice**
- G2 reviews: `site:g2.com [competitor]` — read the 3-star reviews, not the 5-stars
- Trustpilot, Capterra
- What customers praise vs. what they complain about

**Pricing & Packaging**
- Pricing page (if public)
- Trial / freemium model
- Enterprise vs. SMB focus

## Phase 2 — Ongoing Monitoring (Weekly)
Set up regular checks on:
- New blog posts / content pieces
- LinkedIn posts from founders and key staff
- New G2 reviews
- Job postings (reveals strategic direction)
- Funding announcements

## Deliverable Format

```
## [Competitor Name]
**Positioning:** [one-sentence summary]
**ICP:** [who they target]
**Core Value Claim:** [their main promise]
**Strengths:** [what they do well]
**Weaknesses / Gaps:** [what customers complain about; what they ignore]
**Positioning Opportunity for Us:** [specific gap we can exploit]
```

## Turning Intel Into GTM Advantage
- Weaknesses in G2 reviews = exact pain language to use in outreach
- Topics they ignore = content opportunities to own
- Their ICP = a starting point to test, then differentiate from
- Their pricing = anchoring data for your own pricing conversations
